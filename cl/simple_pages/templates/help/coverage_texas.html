Texas State Court Coverage — What Does CourtListener Have?

CourtListener has a deep, ongoing collection of docket data from the Texas Supreme Court and every Texas appellate court.

Our Texas collection covers cases, docket entries, and documents — including PDFs, WordPerfect files, HTML records, and audio recordings — from the state's appellate court system. The data goes back roughly a century and is refreshed continuously.

Data Sources

Texas publishes docket information through a centralized appellate case management system called TAMES (Texas Appeals Management and e-Filing System). We collect data from TAMES in two complementary ways:

  1. The TAMES website. Our scraper visits the public TAMES pages for each Texas appellate court and pulls down new and updated cases, entries, and documents.

  2. TAMES case-mail emails. TAMES sends notification emails about case activity. We receive these emails, parse them, and feed the updates back into our database to keep cases current in close to real time.

How We Keep It Current

Our Texas scraper runs every hour, looking for new cases and new activity on existing cases. In practice, TAMES tends to publish a fresh batch of updates roughly once a day, so most new activity shows up in CourtListener within a day of being filed.

On top of the scraper, the TAMES notification emails give us a second channel for picking up updates — they also generally arrive about once a day — so cases stay current even when the website changes between scrapes.

What We Have

Our Texas docket collection covers cases from 1900 to the present, and currently contains:

  • Approximately 750,000 cases from the Texas Supreme Court and the state's appellate courts
  • Over 13 million docket entries
  • More than 3.7 million documents, including PDFs, WordPerfect files, HTML records, and MP3 audio recordings

Availability and Roadmap

This data is currently available for database replication through Free Law Project. It is not yet integrated into CourtListener's search, the public REST API, alerts, or our other end-user services.

Bringing Texas state court data into search, the API, alerts, and the rest of CourtListener is on our roadmap. If you are interested in using this data or in helping fund the work to make it more broadly available, please get in touch.
