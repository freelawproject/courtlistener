Help with Alerts on CourtListener

CourtListener was created in 2009 as an alert and awareness tool for people that were interested in keeping up with new cases or topics in the federal circuit courts.

We currently have three kinds of alerts: Search Alerts, RECAP Alerts for PACER, and Citation Alerts.

RECAP Alerts for PACER

RECAP Alerts are a way of keeping up with cases in the PACER federal court filing system. These alerts monitor tens of thousands of cases across the country for new docket entries and send an email whenever new entries are found. In the last 24 hours, {{ d_update_count|intcomma }} dockets and {{ de_update_count|intcomma }} docket entries were updated.

The sources for these alerts are as described in detail on our coverage page.

For active cases, alerts can come within seconds of a new filing. For less active cases, it can take more time or alerts may not arrive at all, if we do not have a way of getting updates for the case.

For closed cases, we get new content and can send alerts when we do client work on those cases or when a RECAP extension user shares a docket with us.

Limitations on RECAP Alerts

As a non-profit, we try to provide our services to as many people as possible. We currently allow {{ MAX_FREE_DOCKET_ALERTS }} docket alerts for free, and give a bonus of {{ DOCKET_ALERT_RECAP_BONUS }} alerts to anybody with the RECAP Extension installed.

Monthly donors can create as many alerts as they need, though we may set up some reasonable limits in the future, based on usage.

Corporate rates are also available for large organizations needing to provide alerts to many users.

We also can sometimes provide need-based exceptions to these rules. If you might need an exception, please let us know. This feature is intended for individual users. If you think you may need to use alerts for a commercial endeavor, please get in touch.

Search Alerts

Search alerts are triggered by our search engine and are thus a powerful way to get fine-tuned alerts on cases or topics that you are following.

Creating Alerts

Creating a search alert is usually straightforward. Simply do a search in CourtListener's Case Law or Oral Argument sections and then click the bell icon in the search bar () or click the Get Alerts button in the sidebar on the left.

After you click the bell icon () or the Get Alerts button, you will see a pop-up like this, where you can see the filters applied to your alert, how many results it had over the past 100 days, and give it a name and frequency.

The available rates are "Real Time", "Daily", "Weekly", "Monthly", or "Off". Real Time alerts are usually delivered within about an hour of when something is published by the court. Daily, weekly, and monthly alerts come at the end of the day, week, and month.

Alerts that are Off will not be run. This can be useful for temporarily disabling an alert.

Editing or Deleting an Alert

Existing alerts can be edited or deleted from your user profile. By clicking the Edit button, you will be taken back to the search screen with the alert configured for editing. There, you can refine your search, or the name or frequency of the alert.

From your profile page, you can also delete an alert.

Disabling an Alert

If you wish to temporarily disable an alert, you can edit it and set the rate to "Off". Every alert email also has a one-click link for disabling the alert that triggered it.

Supported Jurisdictions

Alerts are available for many jurisdictions across the country, and we are frequently expanding them to support even more courts. To see which courts are currently supported, check our coverage page where we list the jurisdictions that we regularly scrape for oral arguments or opinions.

If there is a jurisdiction that is not currently listed, please let us know you are interested in it, and we will do our best to add it to our list.

Citation Alerts

Citation alerts are a way to keep up with citations to a case of interest. When you have a citation alert, you will get an email whenever a new case cites that case. Like regular search alerts described above, citation alerts can be configured with custom queries, filters, or jurisdictions.

To create a citation alert, start at webpage for the case that interests you. In this example, we will use Roe v. Wade.

On that page, in the sidebar on the left, any cases that cite it are listed, and there is a button to "Get Citation Alerts":

Clicking that button will perform a search in CourtListener that shows the case law that cites Roe v. Wade, and a dialog will appear where you can easily save the alert.

If you prefer to modify the alert, you can close the dialog that popped up, tweak the query and filters you wish to apply, and then save it as you would any regular alert.

For more on citation alerts, see our blog post announcing this feature.

Coming Soon
