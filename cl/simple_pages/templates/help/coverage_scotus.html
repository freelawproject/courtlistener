Supreme Court Docket Coverage — What Does CourtListener Have?

CourtListener has a comprehensive collection of docket data from the Supreme Court of the United States.

Our SCOTUS collection covers the cases, docket entries, and documents that the Court publishes on its website. We complement that information with case notifications the Court emails to subscribers, so the data in CourtListener stays in step with the Court's own systems.

This is not the same as our case law collection, which focuses on the Court's published opinions. The pages described here are the underlying dockets — the running record of activity in each case, including filings, orders, motions, and briefs.

Data Sources

We get SCOTUS data from two places, both operated by the Court itself:

  1. The Supreme Court's docket website. The Court publishes case dockets and filings on supremecourt.gov. Our scraper visits these pages and pulls down new and updated content.

  2. Case-update emails. The Court sends notification emails when there is activity on a case. We receive these notifications, parse them, and feed the updates back into our database so cases stay current in close to real time.

How We Keep It Current

Our SCOTUS scraper runs every hour, looking for new cases and new activity on existing cases.

On top of that hourly cycle, the email notifications give us near real-time updates whenever the Court publishes a new entry. Together, these two systems let CourtListener mirror the Supreme Court's docket as closely as possible.

What We Have

Our SCOTUS docket collection covers cases from 2000 to the present, and currently contains:

  • Nearly 200,000 cases
  • Close to 1 million docket entries
  • More than 300,000 documents downloaded from the Court (PDFs and other filings)

A very small number of documents — currently in the low hundreds — were referenced by the Court but were not retrievable when we tried to download them. We re-check these on an ongoing basis.

Availability and Roadmap

This data is currently available for database replication through Free Law Project. It is not yet integrated into CourtListener's search, the public REST API, alerts, or our other end-user services.

Bringing SCOTUS docket data into search, the API, alerts, and the rest of CourtListener is on our roadmap. If you are interested in using this data or in helping fund the work to make it more broadly available, please get in touch.
